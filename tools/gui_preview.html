The panel renders pixel-for-pixel from the firmware draw code: the SWEEP SETTINGS screen with the arm animation underneath and the live status bar on the right. Editing a value shows the effect immediately — wafer circle size (sized to the 150 mm max and cropped top/bottom), sweep extent (Edge↔(•) vs Edge↔Edge), the park tick, and the red arm arrow. The arm speed follows the cycle time and speed profile; the generator runs only over the wafer, where a pulsing ⚡ appears next to the arrow. Use Tour shows to pick the demonstrated features and Play speed to scale the tour; each value runs for a few full oscillations and only changes when the arm is at the wafer edge. To record a GIF, capture the panel (Windows Win+Alt+R or ScreenToGif). The second panel shows the hidden hardware SETUP menu — now 20 rows that scroll vertically with a sliding 6-row window, a row/total counter and ▲/▼ markers. Drag Setup row to scroll: it includes the new gear ratio (Gear in / Gear out, default 15:108), Parkspd (arm positioning speed in deg/s for homing/park/staging), Accel/Jerk, Backlash take-up (µsteps), the driver RunHold/PrkHold %, Chop (StealthChop/SpreadCycle) and Interp (microstep interpolation), and Stall/StallSG (StallGuard collision detection → park + red COLLISION on a blocked arm). The About screen is omitted.
